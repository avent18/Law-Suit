Thiruvananthapuram: The Supreme Court's May 16, 2025 judgment invalidating post-facto environmental clearances has delivered a significant blow to the Outer Ring Road (ORR) project in the city.

The National Highways Authority of India (NHAI) is awaiting environmental clearance (EC) for the northern section of the ORR, between Thekkada and Navaikulam. The issue is compounded by the expiration of the State Environmental Impact Assessment Authority (SEIAA) in March 2025. Until a new committee is formed, the responsibility lies with the Union ministry, which can no longer issue post-facto clearances due to the Supreme Court's decision.

"This judgment has created an unexpected legal bottleneck," said a senior NHAI official. "We were ready to initiate compensation, but we are not legally permitted to proceed without clearance."

The Rs 4,767-crore ORR project, sanctioned in August 2024 and spanning 63 km, aims to decongest traffic and improve connectivity to the Vizhinjam International Seaport. Construction was slated to begin in April 2025 but now faces indefinite delays.

"This is a case of administrative delay compounding a legal issue," said S Chandramohan Nair, convener of the action council for affected landowners. "People have waited for months for compensation, and now we're back to square one."
